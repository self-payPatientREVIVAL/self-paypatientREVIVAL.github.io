On Health-Insurance

Back in the Philippines, the "Universal Health Care Act" (Republic Act 11223) became law on 21 February 2019. So while private health-insurace options do exist, all PH citizens are declared to be members of the government owned or controlled corporation (G.O.C.C.) Philippine Health Insurance Corp. (PhilHealth). Of course, laws are more easily made than implemented, in a non-1st-world republic with a limited budget to administer over 7k-2k islands (depending on hightide or lowtide).

To fill-in the gaps, the Department of Social Welfare & Development (D.S.W.D.) hosts "Malasakit Centers" to provide further financial-support/"Assistance to Individuals in Crisis Situations (A.I.C.S.)", as best it can. On Alternative & Complementary Medicine

The ‚ÄúTraditional and Alternative Medicine Act (TAMA) of 1997‚Äù (Republic Act 8423) was signed into law on 7 December 1997, creating the Philippine Institute of Traditional and Alternative Health Care (P.I.T.A.H.C.), a government owned or controlled corporation (G.O.C.C.) under the Department of Health (D.O.H.). As of 14 December 2024, there are 1,530 listed certified practitioners & 119 listed accredited facilities/organizations.

On Medical Tourism

A note: While medical-tourism to the Philippines is a thing (and here's a write-up about it from the U.S. Department of Commerce), it's generally most advisable to seek treatment from facilities inside the National Capital Region (a.k.a. "Metro Manila"), like from St. Luke's Medical Center, or in specilaist-centers in major-cities. Healthcare services in far-flung provincial areas may prove very basic.

On Telemedicine (for Filipinos, Domestic & Abroad)

Metro Pacific Investments Corp.'s "mWell PH"-app has a partnership with the Department of Migrant Workers (D.M.W.) to ensure Filipinos EVERYWHERE can get affordable healthcare, as well as (self-reportedly) both accreditation from the Departent of Health (D.O.H.) & partnerships with local government units (L.G.U.'s).
