Now that the caveats have been mentioned, how do H.S.M.'s work?
  • Since they're legally NOT INSURANCE, insurance-terms are not be used with them. Hence, each ministry has its own terminology. For example, premiums are't collected by L.H.S.: "monthly contrubutions" are. The said ministry has no insurance-plan with any deductables, but an "Annual Unshared Amount (A.U.M.)" does exist with each level of membership offered.
  • The system runs on altruism, mutual-charity, and religious obligaiton, as opposed to legal entitlements.

Has the last point been abused before? You bet! (Recently, bankruptcy was filed by a so-called "Trinity HealthShare" that the Alliance has rightly called a "sham" after being found guilty of fraud.) Be careful who gets awarded your money!

A note on L.H.S.: Things have been rocky in L.H.S. with since 2020, and I'd like to compare notes with members of other H.S.M.'s, if possible. How are thing in your ministry? Taking the numbers reported in the members' e-mail newsletters and squeezing as much as I can from them on spreadsheets, I got this:

  • When I joined in 2014, contribtuions & shared bills were practically 1:1, and just under $60k per month. In just 1 year, that jumped to $1mil. We, the membership, were healthy & health-conscious people. BUT mainstream news-media started catching-on and raising awareness of H.S.M.'s existence. Wider society joined en masse! By 2016, $5mil was normal for L.H.S.'s monthly revenue from July onward. It was 2017, when the troubles began, with multi-million dollar monthly deficits appearing every do often. Not a problem, apparently, since monthly contributions of $20mil were starting, too. Big money! What could possibly go wrong? By 2018, the monthly-rate was hiked by June (AFTER deficits of $10mil & approaching were added to monthly deficits), so contribution did jump to the 1x-maximum of $48mil by December. For 2019, monthly revenue averaged $30mil, it seemed progress was made to address the backlog‚Ä¶ And then, COVID attacked! From 2020-2021, the contrubution-rate was raised again, top-leadership was replaced, and the membership-offerings were overhauled. The year 2022 was the year debt-elimination was on the forefront. By September 2024, 74% of the backlog was paid-off. As of December 2024, no furhter updates on paying-off the debt has been issued.
